one cannot come into true relation with his fellow man until he develops the habit of hearing the questions in everything they say or do not say. all communication is interrogative, even and especially if it should lack the characteristic marks of interrogation.

imperatives, declaratives, exclamatory outbursts--they are but different sorts of questions, or different ways of inflecting the central question that preoccupies us all. "am I real?" the answer of course being "not as such", which answer our tacit terror prevents us from voicing and which terror only the light upturn at ends, the gentle curve around points, can distract us from or allay.
