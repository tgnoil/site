the basic precepts of buddhism seem to me to be present in just three of the many (many) intricately braided sets, series, groups, and progressions that comprise its fantastically systematic and numerological doctrines. they are: the four noble truths (life is suffering, suffering is desire, desire can be ended, the way to end it is the eightfold path), the eightfold path (wisdom group: right view, right intention; moral discipline group: right speech, right action, right livelihood; concentration group: right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration) and the defilements (greed, aversion, and delusion, with ignorance at the root)
